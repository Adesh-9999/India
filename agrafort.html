🏰 Agra Fort – The Walled City of Mughal Majesty

Just 2.5 km from the Taj Mahal, the Agra Fort stands as a powerful symbol of Mughal strength and architectural brilliance. This red sandstone fortress was not only a military base but also the residence of emperors, echoing centuries of power, betrayal, and royal intrigue.

🏯 Why Agra Fort is More Than Just a Fort

  • 🧱 Built by Emperor Akbar in 1573 using red sandstone
  • 👑 Served as the main residence of the Mughal emperors until 1638
  • 🌕 Contains stunning palaces like Jahangir’s Palace, Khas Mahal, and Shish Mahal
  • 🔒 Shah Jahan, the creator of the Taj Mahal, was imprisoned here by his own son

📖 A Fort Full of Stories

Behind its massive gates, Agra Fort hides delicate marble pavilions, mirror-inlaid halls, and echoes of history. One can still stand at Musamman Burj, the octagonal tower where Shah Jahan gazed at the Taj Mahal in his final years — a love story etched in stone.

📸 Visual Journey

🧭 Visiting Tips

- Open daily from sunrise to sunset (except some national holidays)
- Entry fee: ₹50 (Indians), ₹650 (Foreigners)
- Wear comfortable footwear — the fort is large and needs walking
- Hire a guide or audio tour to unlock fascinating stories

📍 A Fortress That Witnessed Empires Rise & Fall

Agra Fort is not just a historical monument — it’s a storyteller in stone. Each corridor, arch, and dome holds the weight of Mughal legacy. It’s a must-visit for anyone looking to go beyond the Taj and into the heart of imperial India.
