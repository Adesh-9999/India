🌬️ Hawa Mahal – The Palace of Winds

Rising like a pink crown in the heart of Jaipur, the Hawa Mahal is one of India’s most iconic palaces. Built in 1799 by Maharaja Sawai Pratap Singh, this five-storey marvel was designed so royal women could observe street festivals without being seen — all while enjoying the cool breeze flowing through its 953 jharokhas (small windows).

🏰 Architectural Beauty in Every Breeze

  • 🌸 Built with red and pink sandstone, blending Rajput and Mughal styles
  • 🌬️ The 953 intricately carved windows allow natural ventilation
  • 👀 Designed for royal ladies to watch the city while remaining unseen
  • 🌇 Offers breathtaking sunrise views from the top floors

🏛️ A Palace With Purpose

More than just a pretty façade, the Hawa Mahal was a symbol of tradition, privacy, and clever engineering. Its honeycomb structure not only reflects beauty but also keeps the palace cool — even during Jaipur’s scorching summers.

📸 Visual Journey

🧭 Visiting Tips

- Best visited early in the morning for soft lighting and fewer crowds
- Entry fee: ₹50 (Indians), ₹200 (Foreigners)
- Bring water — there are lots of stairs inside
- The City Palace and Jantar Mantar are nearby — plan a full day!

💖 A Breeze from the Past

Hawa Mahal isn’t just a palace — it’s a living sculpture, carved from history and wind. Standing in its shadow or looking out from its windows, you don’t just see Jaipur — you feel it.
