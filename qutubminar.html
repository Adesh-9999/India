🕌 Qutub Minar – Towering Through Time

Rising 73 meters into the sky, the Qutub Minar is not just Delhi’s pride but also one of India’s most remarkable historical structures. Built in the early 13th century by Qutb-ud-din Aibak and later completed by his successors, this towering minaret is a blend of Indo-Islamic architecture and an enduring symbol of India’s rich medieval past.

🏛️ Architecture & Legacy

  • 🧱 Built from red sandstone and marble
  • 🔠 Inscribed with intricate Arabic calligraphy
  • 🏗️ Five distinct storeys, each marked by a projecting balcony
  • 📜 Part of the Qutub Complex – a UNESCO World Heritage Site

🕰️ A Walk Through History

Qutub Minar marks the beginning of Muslim rule in India. Nearby ruins, tombs, and mosques whisper stories of dynasties long gone. Don’t miss the famous Iron Pillar, standing rust-free for over 1600 years — still a mystery for scientists.

📸 Visual Journey

🧭 Visiting Tips

- Best visited in early morning or just before sunset
- Entry is ticketed (₹40 for Indians, ₹600 for foreigners)
- Closed at night; photography is allowed
- Wear comfortable shoes – the complex is large and full of history

📍 A Historic Landmark That Touches the Sky

The Qutub Minar isn’t just an ancient tower — it’s a monument that has stood tall through centuries of change. Visiting it is like flipping through the pages of Delhi’s past, one carved stone at a time.
